Gaussian beam expansion

The scattered electron has an angular distribution,
$$ f \left( \theta \right)=\sum_{i=1}^{N}{A\ _{i}\ e^{-B\ _{i}\ \theta^{2}}} \tag{5} $$ To make sure all components are positive, I changed the expansion expression,
$$ f \left( \theta \right)=\sum_{i=1}^{N}{A\ _{i}^{2}\ e^{-B\ _{i}\ \theta^{2}}} \tag{6} $$ The coefficient \(B_i\) is a function of electron energy. To find the optimized \(A_i\), which can represent the function, we can define an error function at the beginning,
$$ Q=\int_{}^{}{d\theta\ }{\left(f \left( \theta \right)-\sum_{i=1}^{N}{A\ _{i}^{2}\ e^{-B\ _{i}\ \theta^{2}}}\right)^{2}} \tag{7} $$ Then I used gradient descent to minimize the error function. The algorithm can be found from wiki(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gradient_descent).
